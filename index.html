Chocolate Chip Cookies

Ingredients

  • 5 c. flour
  • 2 tsp. baking soda
  • 2 tsp. salt
  • 2 c. butter or shortening
  • 2 tsp. vanilla
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 pkg. chocolate chips

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees
  2. Combine flour, baking soda, and salt in a small bowl
  3. Beat butter, sugars, vanilla, and eggs in a large bowl until creamy
  4. Gradually beat in flour mixture
  5. Stir in chocolate chips
  6. Drop by teaspoonfuls on ungreased baking sheet
  7. Bake for nine to eleven minutes

Sugar Cookies

Ingredients

  • 1 c. butter
  • 1 c. sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp. vanilla
  • 1 t. milk
  • 3 c. flour
  • 3 tsp. baking powder

Steps

  1. Cream butter and add sugar gradually
  2. Add milk and vanilla and cream well
  3. Sift flour and baking powder together
  4. Add flour and baking powder to milk and vanilla
  5. Chill for 10 minutes
  6. Preheat oven to 400 degrees
  7. Roll and cut into desired shapes
  8. Back until very light brown

Notes: I would recommend baking for eleven minutes.

Snickerdoodle Cookies

Ingredients

  • 2 3/4 c. sifted flour
  • 3 t. baking powder
  • 1/2 t. salt
  • 1 c. butter
  • 1 1/2 c. sugar
  • 2 eggs

Coating Ingredients

  • 4 t. sugar
  • 4 t. cinnamon

Steps

  1. Mix and sift flour, baking powder, and salt
  2. Cream butter, add sugar gradually and cream until fluffy
  3. Add beaten eggs and mix
  4. Add sifted dry ingredients gradually and mix
  5. Chill in refrigerator
  6. Mold dough into small balls using 1 t. dough for each ball and roll in mixture of remaining sugar and cinnamon
  7. Bake about 2 inches apart on ungreased baking sheets in a preheated 400 degree oven for about 10 minutes
  8. Store in covered container

Soft Sugar Cookies

Ingredients

  • 1 c. sugar
  • 1/2 c. butter, softened to room temp
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 c. sour cream
  • 2 tsp. vanilla extract
  • 3 c. all purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp. baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp. baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp. salt

Frosting Ingredients

  • 2 c. powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp. vanilla extract
  • 1-2 t. milk
  • 1/2 c. butter, softened to room temp

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 350 degrees
  2. With your mixer cream together 1/2 c. butter with 1 c. sugar and mix for a couple of minutes until smooth
  3. Add in sour cream, eggs, and vanilla and mix well
  4. In a large bowl sift together flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt
  5. Slowly add the dry ingredients into the wet and mix well
  6. Measure three t. of dough for each cookie. Use your hands to roll three t. of cookie dough into a smooth ball
  7. Place on ungreased cookie sheets, making sure to leave about 1 inch between each cookie. Using your hand to lightly flatten the dough a bit.
  8. Bake for 11-12 minutes, then immediately remove from the oven.
  9. Carefully transfer the cookies to a cooling rack.

Frosting

  1. Beat butter with a mixer for about 30 seconds
  2. Add in powdered sugar, one cup at a time
  3. Add in vanilla and 1-2 t. of milk
  4. mix until light and fluffy

Shortbread Cookies

Ingredients

  • 3/4 pound unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1 c. sugar, plus extra for sprinkling
  • 1 tsp. pure vanilla extract
  • 3 1/2 c. all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp. salt
  • 6 to 7 ounces very good semisweet chocolate, finely chopped

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees
  2. Mix together the butter and 1 c. of sugar until they are just combined and add the vanilla
  3. Sift together the flour and salt, then add them to the butter-and-sugar mixture.
  4. Mix on low speed until the dough starts to come together. Dump onto a surface dusted with flour and shape into a flat disk. Wrap in plastic and chill for 30 minutes.
  5. Roll the dough 1/2-inch thick and cut with a 3 by 1-inch finger-shaped cutter. Place the cookies on an ungreased baking sheet and sprinkle with sugar.
  6. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes, until the edges begin to brown. Allow to cool to room temperature.
  7. When the cookies are cool, place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Put 3 ounces of the chocolate in a glass bowl and microwave on high power for 30 seconds.
  8. Stir with a wooden spoon. Continue to heat and stir in 30-second increments until the chocolate is just melted.
  9. Add the remaining chocolate and allow it to sit at room temperature, stirring often, until it's completely smooth. Stir vigorously until the chocolate is smooth and slightly cooled; stirring makes it glossier.
  10. Drizzle 1/2 of each cookie with just enough chocolate to coat it.
